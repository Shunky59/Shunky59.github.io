Electrical Engineering Component Sourcing Fabrication

Research, Sourcing, and Fabrication

I needed a reliable way to commute to my job at Robot Revolution, but I didn't want to just buy an off-the-shelf solution. I wanted to understand the mechanics of electric propulsion. I started by researching the necessary components—voltage requirements, motor KV ratings, and controller amperage limits—to achieve the performance I was looking for.

I sourced the individual parts from various vendors on eBay to keep costs down while maximizing power. The build process involved stripping down a standard mountain bike frame and retrofitting it with a hub motor and battery system. I handled all the wiring myself, soldering the high-current connections and designing a layout to mount the controller securely to the frame.

Performance & Commute

The result was a machine far more capable than standard consumer e-bikes. The system delivers enough torque to reach speeds of approximately 30 mph on pure electric power, with no pedal assistance required. This drastically cut down my commute time.

Reliability was key. Because I relied on this bike to get to work every day, the soldered connections had to be vibration-proof and the battery mounting had to be robust enough to handle road bumps at speed. It served as my daily driver for the entire summer.
