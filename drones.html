Custom FPV Builds

My journey into First Person View (FPV) drones began with a desire to understand the intersection of high-speed aerodynamics, radio frequency (RF) transmission, and power systems. I design, build, and pilot custom quadcopters optimized for both freestyle flying and cinematic capture.

The build process involves selecting components for optimal power-to-weight ratios, detailed soldering of flight controllers (FC) and electronic speed controllers (ESC), and configuring firmware via Betaflight. I manually tune PID loops to eliminate prop-wash oscillation and ensure locked-in flight characteristics.

Educational Curriculum: Robot Revolution

Beyond personal engineering, I applied my expertise to design and launch a comprehensive "Build Your Own Drone" course for Robot Revolution. The goal was to translate complex avionics concepts into an accessible curriculum for middle and high school students.

Curriculum Development

I structured the course to guide students through the entire engineering lifecycle of a drone. This was not a kit-assembly class; students learned the fundamental theory behind every component they touched.

  • Electronics Fabrication: Taught precision soldering techniques for attaching motors to ESCs and wiring flight stacks.
  • Flight Dynamics: Explained the physics of multi-rotor flight, including yaw, pitch, roll, and throttle mixing.
  • Safety & Regulations: Integrated modules on LiPo battery safety (charging/storage) and FAA regulations for recreational UAV pilots.

Project Takeaways

Operating in the FPV space has sharpened my ability to troubleshoot complex electro-mechanical systems in the field. Translating that knowledge into a curriculum for students reinforced my communication skills and my ability to break down technical barriers for non-engineers.

  • Systems Integration: gained deep experience ensuring compatibility between video transmitters (VTX), receiver protocols (ELRS/Crossfire), and flight logic.
  • Diagnostics: Learned to analyze blackbox logs to diagnose noise issues and filter settings.
  • Technical Leadership: Successfully managed a classroom environment, ensuring safety while fostering enthusiasm for STEM.
