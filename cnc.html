The Machine: Shapeoko XXL

To expand my fabrication capabilities beyond 3D printing, I integrated a Shapeoko XXL CNC router into my workshop. This 3-axis machine allows me to work with wood, plastics, and aluminum, offering much larger build volume and structural strength than additive methods.

Safety and workflow efficiency were top priorities. I built a custom enclosure based on open-source plans to manage dust and noise. I then wired a custom control panel directly into the enclosure's frame. It features dedicated switching for the router, shop vacuum, and lighting, along with a hard-wired emergency stop button for immediate system cutoff.

The Shapeoko XXL inside the custom sound-dampened enclosure with integrated power controls.

Projects & Applications

I utilize this machine for both functional joinery and precision art. The workflow typically involves modeling in Fusion 360 or Carbide Create, defining toolpaths (CAM), and executing the g-code. I have experimented heavily with V-carving bits for detailed graphic relief work.
