The Machine: Build & Evolution

My 3D printing experience is anchored by this machine, which I use for all of my personal prototyping and modeling projects. The journey began when I built a Prusa MK3s from a kit to understand the underlying motion system. I later expanded its capabilities by adding the MMU2 (Multi-Material Upgrade).

Recently, I performed a comprehensive overhaul, upgrading the entire system to the MK4s with the MMU3. To enable remote control and video monitoring, I integrated a Raspberry Pi running Octoprint. This allows me to manage prints from anywhere and capture time-lapses of complex builds.

My current workstation setup with the Prusa MK4s, soldering station, and electronics bench.

Enclosure & Environment

To improve air quality and print consistency, I constructed an enclosure based on a popular open-source community design. While I primarily print with PETG for its durability and ease of use, the enclosure provides a stable thermal environment that improves layer adhesion.

More importantly, the enclosure helps with air quality management in my workspace. I integrated a filtration and fume extraction system to ensure a safe environment during long prototyping sessions.

The enclosure setup featuring LED lighting and fume extraction.

Mechatronics: Pen Plotter Conversion

I wanted to learn more about CNC motion paths beyond standard filament printing. To do this I implemented a modular attachment that converts my 3D printer into a precision 2D pen plotter. I printed a custom pen carriage that snaps securely onto the print head and can hold various drafting tools.

The biggest challenge was actually the software. I had to manually modify the G-code generation scripts to change how the printer handles "Z-hop" movements. Instead of the standard filament retraction the printer now physically lifts the pen off the paper between drawing strokes to prevent stray ink lines.

Pen plotter installed on the 3D Printer.

Impact: COVID-19 Face Shield Response

In March 2020 when PPE was scarce I converted my workshop into a 24/7 manufacturing line. I utilized the open-source Prusa Face Shield design and optimized the print settings to maximize production speed without sacrificing safety. I ran the printer continuously for weeks to meet the demand.

I coordinated directly with local medical professionals to distribute the equipment where it was needed most. Recipients included Summit EMS, Overlook Hospital, Morristown Hospital, as well as local doctors and orthodontists.

Lehigh Formula SAE Support

While I was a member of the Lehigh Formula SAE team, I took charge of the 3D printing efforts for the shop. The photo below shows a different machine I operated in the university lab.

In this specific instance, I was printing custom welding jigs (mounts) for the chassis team. These fixtures were critical for holding the steel frame tubes in precise alignment during the welding process, ensuring the chassis met the strict geometric tolerances required for the competition vehicle.
