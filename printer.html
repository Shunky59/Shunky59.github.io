Prusa MK4s + MMU3 SolidWorks G-Code Modification

The Setup & Applications

My workshop centers around a Prusa MK4s equipped with the MMU3 multi-material unit. Early on I realized that printing high-performance materials like ABS in a standard room resulted in warping due to drafts and temperature fluctuations. To solve this I designed and built a custom thermal enclosure which maintains a stable internal temperature. I also integrated a fume extraction system so I can safely run long prints in my workspace without air quality issues.

This isn't just a hobby machine. It is my primary manufacturing tool for engineering projects. I used this rig to prototype and produce functional suspension components for the Lehigh Formula SAE car. Being able to print parts overnight allowed our team to test fitment and geometry rapidly before committing to expensive metal machining.

Mechatronics: Pen Plotter Conversion

I wanted to learn more about CNC motion paths beyond standard filament printing. To do this I designed a modular attachment that converts my 3D printer into a precision 2D pen plotter. I modeled a custom carriage in Fusion 360 that snaps securely onto the print head and can hold various drafting tools.

The biggest challenge was actually the software. I had to manually modify the G-code generation scripts to change how the printer handles "Z-hop" movements. Instead of the standard filament retraction the printer now physically lifts the pen off the paper between drawing strokes to prevent stray ink lines.

Impact: COVID-19 Face Shield Response

In March 2020 when PPE was scarce I converted my workshop into a 24/7 manufacturing line. I utilized the open-source Prusa Face Shield design and optimized the print settings to maximize production speed without sacrificing safety. I ran the printer continuously for weeks to meet the demand.

I coordinated directly with hospital administrators to deliver over 400 face shields to front-line workers at Overlook and Morristown Medical Centers as well as the Summit Emergency Squad.
